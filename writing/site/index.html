About This Site...

What and Why

This site exists to share content online. It has a few opinions driving my choices of how I am presenting the content:


  1. Content should be authored separately from how it is viewed. Writing and reading are two different things, so trying to merge them both into one app serves neither audience.
  2. The focus of the web should be on what is said, not how it looks. I do believe that the looks of an app matters... but it should not be the primary focus. Frankly, I'd be in favor of every site looking and working the same, so we can focus on content and functionality.
  3. Bright colors hurt some people's eyes. Dark colors are hard for other people. We should not pick one of those extremes at all. This site sits in the middle, so should be friendly to all eyes.
  4. Audiences do not want to click through pages after pages of your content. Content should be presented in a way that makes it easy to navigate, and organized by topic. Not hashtags, but an actual on-screen list of topics. (Hashtags are another way that the web went of the rails.) Once you get into a topic, you should quickly see as much about it as you can, not have to click through dozens of pages.
  5. No ads. They suck. If you need to monetize your site, do it with affiliate links that are relevant to the content - that way you are not pushing anything on your audience, but you get a little something back in exchange for guiding them towards something they were looking at anyway.


What that all boils down to is this site.


This is the first / worst iteration of it, and all anyone is seeing is the resulting words. There is a whole authoring environment that only runs on my PC to create those words and these pages.


At this point, having written this, it is good enough to be my personal blog. I am undecided if I am going to pursue it any farther to make it useful for other people, put the authoring environment so other people can try it, or anything else. But I have imported a few old blog posts and social media feeds to get a sense of how it works.


The choices to have dates and titles run down the middle with content on either side is deliberate. I want the content to feel like a timeline, so you can get a sense of forward motion in time as you read. For blogs, you can see the author's thoughts evolve. For social media, you see things in the order they were presented. I can see this also working for history timelines, or even creative writing to share a story where the timing has focus. It does get a little wonky at times. It doesn't work well if you resize your browser. I'm OK with that. I'm still noodling the final form of all this.

The Tech Side of this Site

This is not a complex piece of tech:


I have a local app written in Astro JS that I kick off from the command line on my PC. This provides me with a local version of this web site where I can edit everything on this site via my web browser. It looks fairly similar to the public site, but with all kinds of editing widgets added.


When I make changes, they are all written to .json files on my system via API calls. I then run the build process that Astro provides, and get a version of everything as static HTML, which I then upload to my repository on github, which automatically publishes them to codingdave.com


That is it - no databases, nothing fancy, just a small pile of vanilla Javascript spaghetti. Most of the code is visible on the public site because I have not bothered to make it hide itself during the build process. Maybe I should. It is currently not elegant. (As in, nobody would read this code and hire me because of it.) But for now... the site works for me. And that is good enough.
