Sabbatical: Week Five

January 3, 2025 by Dave Armstrong

Am I really five weeks into this break already?

My creative practice is so far a continual exercise in finding things that don't work. But I'm walking towards a vision of how I want monotypes to come out. I'm not loving free-hand inking on glass, and also not loving the consistency of using a brush to paint ink onto glass. So I'm experimenting with different tools, inks, etc. 

I'm also working through my thoughts on block printing. I've done a few blocks and prints this past week, and gotten the prints to come out at least as well as the carving did, but I want a more detailed result than my carving ability/tools allow. Chris Gill implied as much in a couple emails before the holidays, and he was totally right! 

That has led me down some internal thoughts on art vs. craft, modern tools vs. traditional techniques. I tend to land on the side of using whatever modern tech is available to get the result you want. I appreciate hand-crafted works, and I love a process that enables good result (both in art and tech), but I don't like processes that are just tedium when there are more effective ways to get the same result. I also believe that when we look back at artists from past centuries, they used the tools available in their time. They did not arbitrarily reject tools based on artistic philosophy - they used the tools they had to get the result they want. That being the case, I am not going to fret over whether my work is hand-crafted or digitially assisted. I am going to focus on results. 

With all that in mind, when I think about tools to achieve the print results I want, I don't believe my carving skill should be a roadblock. I can design digitally and CNC the carving, leaving my creative inputs as the design, the ink, and the actual final print. I am 100% comfortable with defedning the artistic integrity of that mix, especially since I just as likely to use my tech skills in the automation anyway.

But there is a catch to doing more with CNC - my promise to myself for this sabbatical was that because I do not have an income, I am not going to buy new tools. Which means I need to earn a CNC machine to crave my blocks by selling my work. Which means I need to figure out what I can make that people would actually buy.

And that takes me back to painting. While I'm not an amazing painter, enough people like what I do that I can probably sell some. So I'm going to bring some painting back into the mix to see if I can find the right balance of painting what I enjoy while also creating result people want. 

If anyone is still reading, yes, this week was a lot of thought. Much more comtemplating of a creative practice vs the prior week which was just holidays. 

The other update is on my exercise program and health - that was the primary reason I wanted time off in the first place -- to make sure health came before career for a while. And this last week was bad. I could not exercise much. I gained a ton of weight. My blood sugar was up. And high blood sugar causes food cravings, so I can feel that spiral trying to take me out. So everything was bad, and this next week I need to break the spiral. 

I also did apply for one job. I'm not actively looking to start work again, but I also am aware that perfect jobs are few and far between, so I do have one eye looking out just in case something amazing popped up. And I saw a software job with a small team doing educational work. Not corporate. Not startup. Looks great. I sent a resume. But I did not see it until the job posting had been up for weeks, so I'm not really expecting a call from it. But it is the only thing I've seen so far that would drag me out of my sabbatical. 

So here we go, into 2025!
